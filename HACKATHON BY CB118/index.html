DODO BIRD

the dodo bird (Raphus cucullatus) was a flightless bird endemic to the island of Mauritius in the Indian Ocean. It was about the size of a turkey, with a plump body, small wings, and a large, hooked beak. Its plumage was greyish-brown, and it had a bushy tail.

Wind energy The mammoth (genus Mammuthus) was an extinct group of elephants that roamed the Earth during the Pleistocene and Holocene epochs, roughly 2.6 million to 4,000 years ago. Caspian tiger

The Caspian tiger (Panthera tigris virgata) was a subspecies of tiger that once roamed a vast territory in Central Asia, from eastern Turkey to northern China. Sadly, it is now considered extinct.

Irish deer

The Irish elk, also known as the giant Irish deer or Megaloceros giganteus, was an extinct species of deer that lived during the Pleistocene and Holocene epochs, from about 0.45 million years ago to around 11,000 years ago.
